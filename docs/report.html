2. Phase 1: Scoring Authority Transfer (v2.0 → v2.0.1)

Problem

Users manually assigned 1–10 lens scores via 6 sliders. The AI was told “Do NOT assign numeric scores.” User values flowed directly into the guardrails composite formula.

Impact

  • Dataset = subjective opinion, not analytical output
  • Algorithm echoes user bias instead of computing analysis
  • Stability classification reflects user consistency, not case content
  • Research dataset integrity compromised

3. Phase 2: Canonical Lens Definitions (v2.0.1 → v2.1)

Gap Identified

The v2.0.1 prompt contained generic one-line lens descriptions. Mark's book defines precise operational definitions with enumerated criteria. Without these, the AI has no structured basis for scoring — results are non-reproducible and could drift from the framework.

Canonical Operational Definitions (Now Embedded in Prompt)

1. UTILITARIAN (Consequences)

Evaluate based on overall balance of benefits and harms for all stakeholders, short-term and long-term.
  • Number of individuals affected
  • Severity of benefit or harm
  • Duration of impact
  • Probability and foreseeability of outcomes

2. DEONTOLOGICAL (Duties / Rights)

Evaluate whether the action respects moral duties, professional obligations, and individual rights independent of outcomes.
  • Adherence to professional duties
  • Respect for informed consent
  • Truthful disclosure
  • Avoidance of deception
  • Respect for autonomy and dignity

3. JUSTICE / FAIRNESS

Evaluate whether benefits and burdens are distributed equitably. Assess procedural fairness and impartiality.
  • Equal treatment of similarly situated parties
  • Absence of favoritism
  • Fair access to protections
  • Procedural transparency

4. VIRTUE

Evaluate whether the action reflects professional character traits such as integrity, courage, honesty, and prudence.
  • Integrity under pressure
  • Moral courage
  • Consistency of character
  • Accountability

5. CARE

Evaluate whether the action reflects empathy, relational responsibility, and attentiveness to vulnerability.
  • Protection of vulnerable individuals
  • Recognition of relational impact
  • Responsiveness to harm concerns
  • Demonstrated concern for well-being

6. COMMON GOOD

Evaluate whether the action supports institutional trust, societal welfare, and long-term sustainability beyond individual interests.
  • Institutional trust
  • Public confidence
  • Long-term sustainability
  • Broader community impact

6. Composite Score Formula (Unchanged)

Lens Average = mean(6 AI-computed lens scores)

Lens Component = Lens Average × 0.30

Code Component = Code Compliance Score × 0.70

Composite Score = Lens Component + Code Component

IF violation detected → Composite = min(Composite, 4.9)

8. Validation Test Cases

Three test cases specified by Mark to confirm structural behavior of the v2.1 algorithm.

Test Case 1: Voluntary Noise Reduction Implementation

A manufacturing company identifies employee noise exposure below regulatory limits but associated with long-term hearing damage. The company voluntarily installs acoustic dampening and upgrades PPE. Workers are informed transparently.

Codes: BCSP/ASSP, AIHA/ABIH

Expected: High scores across all lenses (7-10) • Low std deviation (<2.5) • No violations • High composite (>7.0) • Stability: Stable

Test Case 2: Suppressed Exposure Data

An industrial hygienist discovers contaminant measurements exceeding internal guidelines. Management instructs non-disclosure. The hygienist complies and omits data from the report.

Codes: AIHA/ABIH, BCSP/ASSP

Expected: Low Duty/Justice/Care scores (1-4) • Code violation detected (multi_violation) • Composite capped ≤ 4.9 • Stability: Ethically Unstable

Test Case 3: Resource Allocation Trade-Off

A safety director must allocate limited budget between upgrading ventilation at one facility or implementing a company-wide mental health program. Only one can be funded this year.

Codes: BCSP/ASSP, ACOEM

Expected: Divergent lens scores • Higher std deviation (>2.5) or tension • No outright violation • Stability: Contested

9. Implementation Constraints (Locked)

  • Canonical definitions are encoded verbatim in the AI system prompt — no paraphrasing.
  • AI must justify each score with reasoning tied explicitly to operational criteria.
  • Numeric scores remain 1–10, passing through guardrails unchanged except for clamping.
  • No additional lenses may be introduced.
  • No renaming or reinterpretation of lens names without explicit approval.
